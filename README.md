I'm Igor (pronounced *Ee-gor*), a curiosity-driven researcher passionate about the intersection of science and software development. Over the past 10 years in academia, I've worked on computational biology research, developed scientific software, and taught at both schools and universities.

# 🚀 What I Do

Scientists are rarely taught how to write software, yet modern research depends on it. My goal is to help bridge that gap by promoting software engineering principles in academia.

I believe research code should be reproducible, maintainable, and designed to last. I enjoy building scientific tools using modern development practices, clean architecture, testing, automation, and reproducible workflows, making research easier to extend, reuse, and trust.

I've had the opportunity to work in interdisciplinary teams where I've contributed not only through research but also through teaching, mentoring, and software development.
